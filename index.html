Case Study 01 · eBrevia
06 / 23
eBrevia · Legal AI Platform

Prep:
AI Contract
Clustering

Redefining how legal teams scope and price large-scale reviews
Role · Lead Product Designer
Client · Baker McKenzie
Context
07 / 23
Baker McKenzie
Global Law Firm
"We have 5,000 contracts — how do we price this fast?"
Manual estimation · time & cost is guesswork
High uncertainty · error-prone
Redundant work · reviewing nearly identical contracts
Generic AI tools · didn't fit legal workflows
Not scalable · high cognitive load
First Attempt
08 / 23

Auto-grouping
contracts.

Group contracts by AI similarity
Reduce manual review effort
Technically it worked
The Gap
Lawyers could see groups — but couldn't act on them.
Key Insight
09 / 23

The real problem:
decision-making at scale.

Surface Problem
Group contracts faster
→
Actual Problem
Can't estimate effort or price work
Clusters are abstract
No structured grouping logic
No reference point
Nothing to reason from
Hard to estimate effort
Staffing and pricing unclear
Key Insight
Lawyers think in precedents, not categories.
Iterations
10 / 23

From grouping → reference.

Option 01
Generated Title Label
AI generates a brief summary per cluster. Orienting — but still abstract.
Option 02
Playbook Upload
Lawyers upload internal playbooks. AI matches clusters. Powerful — high complexity.
Option 03
Contract Type Tag
Most straightforward reference — but loses nuance.
Solution
11 / 23

Template contract
as anchor.

Each cluster gets one representative contract
Browse the template, understand the pattern
Estimate the rest much faster
Stakeholder Pushback
⚙️ Engineering complexity
⏳ Timeline concerns
⚡ Simpler automation
Reframe
"Would you trust categories, or real examples?"
Outcome · eBrevia
12 / 23

First-to-market.
Real adoption.

2×
ARR
1st
To Market
Baker McKenzie doubled billable usage and contract volume. The template-based approach became one of the core product capabilities.
